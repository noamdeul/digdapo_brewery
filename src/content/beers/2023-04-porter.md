Beer style:
Porter is a dark, rich beer style with deep flavors of chocolate, coffee, caramel, and dried fruits.
Porter got its start in 18th-century London, when a dark, relatively high-alcohol beer became popular among the city's hard-working laborers. One theory holds that the name "porter" was chosen because the porters who carried goods through the city's streets loved to drink this beer after a long workday. The dark, rich beer was not only excellent for relaxation, it also helped them stay warm in the cold seasons. (The word "porter" in English refers to someone who carries goods, i.e., a porter/laborer.)
In the 19th century, with the surge of industrialization and increasingly intense urban life, the beer became an especially popular option for workers, laborers, and city folk. Over time, porter became less popular as other styles developed, and stouts — a heavier version — took its place in the market. In fact, porter was considered the "delicate style" among dark beers.
