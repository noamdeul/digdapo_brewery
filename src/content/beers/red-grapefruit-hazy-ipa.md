## The Story

This beer started as a hop-forward IPA build, but with a softer, juicier direction in mind. The grain bill combines pale ale malt with wheat, oats, and dextrin malt to create a smooth, full body that supports heavy late-hop character and fresh citrus.

Red grapefruit became the centerpiece of the recipe. The peel was added late in the boil for a firm citrus edge, and fresh juice was added in the whirlpool to bring a brighter, more natural grapefruit character. Together with Idaho 7 and Wolf in the whirlpool and dry hop, the result aims for a hazy IPA with bold aroma, soft texture, and a fresh red grapefruit punch.

## Brew Notes

Grain bill:
- 6 kg Pale Ale Malt
- 300 g Dextrin Malt
- 800 g Wheat Malt
- 400 g Flaked Oats

Hop schedule:
- 15 g Magnum at 60 minutes
- 30 g Idaho 7 at whirlpool, 80C
- 30 g Wolf at whirlpool, 80C
- 60 g Idaho 7 dry hop, 3 days before bottling
- 60 g Wolf dry hop, 3 days before bottling

Other additions:
- Peel of 2 red grapefruits, 20 minutes before end of boil
- Fresh juice from 2 red grapefruits at whirlpool, 80C

Yeast:
- LalBrew Windsor

Fermentation was designed to lean toward a soft, fruit-forward profile rather than a dry, sharp IPA. Windsor should leave a fuller body and a slightly English-style character that works nicely with the grapefruit and hazy base.
